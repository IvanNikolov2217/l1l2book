Effect of λ on weight magnitudes
Lambda explorer chart.
Ridge: All five weights shrink smoothly toward zero as λ increases, but none ever reach exactly zero. The model keeps all features — it just relies on them less heavily.
Lasso: Weights snap to exactly zero one by one as λ grows. At λ = 0.1, two of the five features have been eliminated entirely. At λ = 1, only two remain. This is automatic feature selection.

Switch between Ridge and Lasso to see the key difference: Ridge shrinks continuously, Lasso eliminates.
