## Abstract

Regularization is an essential technique in machine learning used to prevent models from overfitting to training data. L1 and L2 regularization represent two of the most widely adopted approaches, each adding a penalty term to the loss function to constrain model complexity. While both methods discourage overly large parameter values, they differ fundamentally in their mathematical formulation and the behavior they produce - L1 promoting sparse solutions by driving certain weights to zero, and L2 distributing penalty more smoothly across all parameters. Choosing between them has meaningful consequences for model interpretability, generalization, and feature selection.
